Siargao, Agusan Del Norte
Why..
I want to visit Siargao, Agusan del Norte because of its stunning tropical beauty, laid-back island vibe, and vibrant surf culture. Known as the "Surfing Capital of the Philippines," Siargao is famous for its world-class waves, especially at Cloud 9, attracting surfers from all over the world. What fascinates me even more is how the island is dominated by foreigners and travelers who have fallen in love with its charm, creating a unique blend of cultures and experiences. Beyond surfing, Siargao offers breathtaking lagoons, white-sand beaches, and a welcoming community that embraces a simple yet fulfilling island lifestyle. The culture there feels different from other places in the Philippines—it's a mix of adventure, relaxation, and an appreciation for nature. I can't wait to explore its beauty, immerse myself in the local scene, and experience what makes Siargao such a special destination.
Things I wanted to:
  • Learn how to surf – Ride the waves at Cloud 9, one of the best surfing spots in the world.
  • Swim in Sugba Lagoon – Enjoy the crystal-clear waters and try paddleboarding or diving from a wooden raft.
  • Island-hop to Naked, Daku, and Guyam Islands – Visit these stunning islands with white sand beaches and turquoise waters.
  • Take a dip in Magpupungko Rock Pools – Experience nature’s natural infinity pools during low tide.
  • Explore Sohoton Cove – Visit the jellyfish sanctuary and marvel at the breathtaking limestone formations.
  • Drive around on a motorbike – Cruise through Siargao’s scenic roads and lush coconut tree landscapes.
  • Watch the sunset at Cloud 9 Boardwalk – Capture the breathtaking view as the sun sets over the ocean.
  • Try local Siargao delicacies – Taste specialties like pan de surf, coconut ice cream, and fresh seafood.
  • Visit Maasin River and its bent palm tree – Swing from the iconic bent palm tree into the peaceful river.
  • Experience the nightlife in General Luna – Enjoy beach parties, live music, and a laid-back island vibe.
Other things I wanted to:
  • Try local cafés – Enjoy a cup of coffee or a smoothie at Siargao’s cozy island-style cafés.
  • Talk to local people and make friends – Experience the warmth of the locals and learn more about island life.
  • Shop for souvenirs – Bring home handmade crafts, surf-inspired accessories, and local products.
  • Find a craft workshop – Join a local weaving, pottery, or art workshop to create unique souvenirs.
  • Join a yoga or wellness retreat – Relax and unwind with yoga sessions by the beach or in a serene jungle setting.
