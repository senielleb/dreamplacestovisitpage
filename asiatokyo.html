Tokyo, Japan
Why..

I want to visit Tokyo, Japan because of its unique blend of tradition and modernity where ancient temples stand alongside futuristic skyscrapers. As a big fan of anime and Japanese movies, I’ve always been fascinated by how Japan’s culture is portrayed in the media, and I want to experience it firsthand. Beyond my love for anime, I’m deeply curious about their way of life, traditions, and the overall atmosphere of the city.

Japan is also known for its deep appreciation of matcha, and as someone who enjoys it, I can't pass up the chance to taste authentic matcha desserts and drinks straight from the source. But more than just one of my personal interests, I want to visit Tokyo to immerse myself in its culture and environment. Whether it's exploring the neon-lit streets of Shibuya, visiting historic temples, or experiencing the tranquility of a Japanese garden, I know Tokyo has so much to offer beyond what I’ve seen on screen. I also want to see the beautiful sakura trees in full bloom and try their famous mochis, experiencing the seasonal charm and flavors that make Japan even more special.

Things I wanted to:
  • Cross the Shibuya Scramble – Experience the world-famous busy crossing in Tokyo.
  • Buy random things from convenience stores and vending machines – Discover unique snacks, drinks, and quirky Japanese items.
  • Eat authentic Japanese food – Try sushi, ramen, takoyaki, okonomiyaki, and other must-eat dishes.
  • Explore Tokyo’s diverse districts – Visit Akihabara for anime, Harajuku for fashion, and Shinjuku for nightlife.
  • Try matcha cafés – Enjoy traditional Japanese matcha desserts, lattes, and sweets.
  • Visit a Japanese shrine or temple – Experience Japan’s rich history.
  • Shop at Don Quijote – Explore Japan’s famous discount store for souvenirs, cosmetics, and quirky items.
  • Watch cherry blossoms in spring – Enjoy the breathtaking beauty of sakura season in parks.
  • Ride a bullet train (Shinkansen) – Experience Japan’s ultra-fast and efficient train travel between major cities.
Other things I wanted to:
  • Buy tons of matcha powder – Stock up on high-quality Japanese matcha for tea, baking, or skincare.
  • Collect anime figurines and gachapon – Visit Akihabara or Nakano Broadway for rare collectibles and capsule toys.
  • Stay in a capsule hotel – Experience Japan’s unique and futuristic compact sleeping pods.
  • Visit a themed café – Enjoy immersive experiences at maid cafés, animal cafés, or anime-themed cafés.
  • Explore a Japanese arcade – Play claw machines, rhythm games, and classic arcade games in places like SEGA or Taito.
