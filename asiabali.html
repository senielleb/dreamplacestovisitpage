Bali, Indonesia
Why..

I want to visit Bali, Indonesia because of its stunning beaches, rich culture, and breathtaking landscapes. Known for its picturesque temples, lush rice terraces, and vibrant beach life, Bali offers a perfect mix of relaxation and adventure. What makes this trip even more meaningful is that my mother also dreams of going there, and I would love to experience it with her.

I’ve heard that Bali shares similarities with the Philippines, from its tropical beauty to its warm and welcoming people, but with a different language and unique cultural influences. That makes me even more curious to explore its traditions, local way of life, and distinct charm. Since I grew up loving the beach, I also want to experience one that’s farther from home, discovering what makes Bali’s shores and island atmosphere truly special.

Things I wanted to do:
  • Relax at Bali’s stunning beaches – Visit famous beaches like Kuta, Seminyak, and Nusa Dua for sunbathing and surfing.
  • Explore Ubud’s rice terraces – Walk through the breathtaking Tegalalang Rice Terraces and enjoy the lush greenery.
  • Visit the iconic Tanah Lot Temple – Witness a beautiful sea temple perched on a rock formation.
  • Try authentic Balinese food – Savor dishes like Babi Guling (suckling pig) and Nasi Goreng (fried rice).
  • Experience Bali’s nightlife – Enjoy beach clubs, rooftop bars, and lively night markets.
  • Swim in Bali’s hidden waterfalls – Discover stunning waterfalls like Sekumpul, Tegenungan, and Gitgit.
  • Take a yoga or wellness retreat – Unwind with yoga, meditation, and spa treatments in Ubud.
  • Snorkel or dive in Nusa Penida – Explore crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and manta rays.
  • Shop at Bali’s traditional markets – Buy handmade crafts, batik textiles, and souvenirs at Ubud Market.
  • Visit the Bali Swing – Capture Instagram-worthy photos while swinging over a jungle or rice field.
Other things I wanted to do:
  • Buy unique Balinese souvenirs – Take home handcrafted wood carvings, silver jewelry, and woven bags.
  • Eat more exotic Balinese food – Try dishes like lawar (spiced minced meat), sate lilit (fish satay), and snake fruit.
  • Visit a traditional Balinese village – Experience local culture in places like Penglipuran or Tenganan Village.
  • Watch a Balinese dance performance – Enjoy cultural shows like the Kecak Fire Dance at Uluwatu Temple.
  • Explore the Sacred Monkey Forest – Walk through an ancient forest sanctuary filled with playful monkeys.
