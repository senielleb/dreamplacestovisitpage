Seoul, South Korea
Why..

I want to visit Seoul, South Korea because of its vibrant city life, rich history, and modern culture. As a fan of Korean entertainment, I’ve watched countless K-dramas, which have made me curious about how life truly is in Korea beyond the screens. While I’m not expecting to meet Koreans as handsome as the K-pop idols I stan, I am excited to explore the breathtaking landmarks the city has to offer, from the majestic Gyeongbokgung Palace to the futuristic streets of Gangnam.

One of my main reasons for visiting is the food. Even though their overall flavor profile isn’t exactly my usual preference, I can’t resist the idea of indulging in authentic Korean barbecue. The experience of grilling meat at the table, wrapping it in fresh lettuce, and pairing it with delicious side dishes is something I can’t wait to try. Seoul is a city that blends tradition and modernity seamlessly, and I look forward to immersing myself in its unique energy, culture, and experiences.

Things I wanted to do:
  • Visit Namsan Tower and put love locks – Capture stunning city views and leave a love lock as a memory.
  • Eat at Myeongdong – Try famous street food like tteokbokki, hotteok, and Korean fried chicken.
  • Wear a hanbok and stroll around – Experience traditional Korean culture by wearing a hanbok while exploring historical sites.
  • Visit a Buddhist temple – Discover the peaceful atmosphere of temples like Jogyesa or Bongeunsa.
  • Try Korean convenience store snacks – Taste unique and delicious treats like instant tteokbokki and banana milk.
  • Stroll around the Han River – Enjoy a scenic walk, rent a bike, or have a picnic by the riverside.
  • Explore Gyeongbokgung Palace – Witness the beauty of Korea’s largest and most famous palace.
  • Shop in Hongdae or Gangnam – Visit trendy fashion stores, K-pop shops, and local boutiques.
  • Experience a Korean BBQ feast – Enjoy an authentic Korean BBQ meal with grilled meats and side dishes.
  • Visit a themed café – Check out unique cafés like animal cafés, flower cafés, or K-pop-inspired cafés.
Other things I wanted to do:
  • Visit entertainment companies – Stop by famous K-pop agency buildings like HYBE, JYP, SM, and YG.
  • Buy K-pop albums and merchandise – Shop for albums, photocard sets, and exclusive merch at lower prices.
  • Visit famous K-drama filming locations – Explore spots like Namsan Stairway, Bukchon Hanok Village, or DDP.
  • Experience a Noraebang (Karaoke Room) – Sing your favorite K-pop songs in a private karaoke room.
  • Explore Insadong’s traditional streets – Discover tea houses, art galleries, and handmade crafts in this cultural district.
